Grammar · Reference

Can, Can't, Must, Mustn't

Study the four modal verbs and their uses. Keep this tab open while you practise!

✅ CAN

ability · possibility
  • I can swim here.
  • We can play chess.
  • She can ride a bike.
Subject + CAN + verb

🚫 CAN'T

impossibility · no permission
  • You can't climb the rocks.
  • We can't park here.
  • I can't run fast yet.
Subject + CAN'T + verb

⚠️ MUST

rules · obligations
  • You must wear a helmet.
  • Hikers must bring a map.
  • We must clean up after.
Subject + MUST + verb

🛑 MUSTN'T

prohibitions · forbidden
  • You mustn't litter here.
  • I mustn't speak in class.
  • We mustn't make a fire.
Subject + MUSTN'T + verb
Key rule: After CAN / CAN'T / MUST / MUSTN'T always use the base form of the verb — no -s, no -ing, no to.

Writing Task

Summer Safety in My Town

Write a short text (5–6 sentences) about what you can, cannot, must and what is forbidden to do in your neighbourhood in summer.

  • A sport or activity you CAN do outside
  • Something you CAN'T do (dangerous or not allowed)
  • A rule people MUST follow (e.g. wearing a helmet)
  • Something that is FORBIDDEN / MUSTN'T be done
  • Your personal opinion about these rules
Bonus ✦  Find one real rule or law about summer safety in your city and write a sentence about it.
