    Today six generations share the same workplaces and cities. The Silent Generation (1928–1945) values discipline and stability. Baby Boomers (1946–1964) grew up during strong economic growth and are known for a powerful work ethic. Generation X (1965–1980) is practical and independent, and is sometimes called the "forgotten generation." Millennials (1981–1996) are digital natives who care about flexibility and work-life balance. Generation Z (1997–2012) grew up with smartphones and is interested in careers linked to technology, such as artificial intelligence and renewable energy. Generation Alpha (2013–now) is the first generation to grow up in a fully digital world.

      Stage 3 · Production

      Opinion Toolkit

      Before you debate, arm yourself with these phrases. Try to use at least one from each column.

      Giving an opinion

      • In my opinion, ...
      • From my point of view, ...
      • As far as I'm concerned, ...

      Agreeing

      • I completely agree that ...
      • That's a fair point.
      • You've got a point there.

      Disagreeing

      • I see your point, but ...
      • I'm not totally convinced, because ...
      • I'd have to disagree, since ...

      Giving a reason

      • This is mainly because ...
      • For example, ...
      • This shows that ...

      Draw a Debate Card

      Press the button to draw a topic. Say whether you agree or disagree, and explain why — using at least two opinion phrases above.

      How to structure your mini debate

      1. State your opinion clearly, using a phrase from the toolkit.
      2. Give one reason and one real-life example to support it.
      3. Consider the opposite view — and respond to it.
